Research Publications

Design and Implementation of New Collision Resistant Fast Hash Function Family for IoT Healthcare Blockchain Ledger
IEEE Conference Publication | 2022

Abstract: This paper presents a novel collision-resistant hash function specifically designed for IoT healthcare blockchain applications. The proposed algorithm enhances ledger security while maintaining efficiency for resource-constrained medical IoT devices. Our implementation demonstrates significant improvements in processing speed and resistance to cryptographic attacks compared to existing solutions.

Design of New Collision Resistant Hash Function for Blockchain in V2V Communication
IEEE SmartNet | 2019

Abstract: This research introduces a specialized hash function for Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) communication systems secured by blockchain technology. The proposed cryptographic solution addresses the unique challenges of high-speed mobile environments, providing enhanced collision resistance and faster verification times for real-time automotive applications.

Blockchain Supervision Strategy for Electronic Health Records
IEEE ICEENG | 2020

Abstract: This paper proposes a novel blockchain-based supervision framework for electronic health records (EHR) that enhances data integrity while maintaining patient privacy. The solution implements a multi-layer access control mechanism and decentralized audit trails, significantly improving compliance with healthcare regulations and preventing unauthorized access to sensitive medical data.

Decentralized IoT Trust Management via Blockchain
IEEE 3ICT | 2020

Abstract: We present a decentralized trust management system for IoT networks using blockchain technology. The solution eliminates single points of failure in device authentication and establishes a reputation-based trust model that dynamically adjusts device privileges based on behavior patterns, significantly enhancing security in large-scale IoT deployments.

Blockchain for Decentralized Emergency Management System
IJCDS | 2023

Abstract: This research implements a blockchain-based emergency management system that coordinates response efforts across multiple agencies without central oversight. The solution provides real-time, tamper-proof information sharing while maintaining data integrity during crisis situations, significantly improving response times and resource allocation efficiency.

DDoS Mitigation in IoT Using Machine Learning and Blockchain Integration
IEEE | 2024

Abstract: This paper presents a hybrid approach combining machine learning algorithms with blockchain technology to detect and mitigate DDoS attacks in IoT environments. The system establishes a decentralized defense mechanism where IoT devices collaboratively identify and isolate malicious traffic patterns, significantly improving network resilience against large-scale coordinated attacks.
